Our Mission

Built on Belief, Not Budget

"Education should reach every child, regardless of where they are born or what their family can afford. That is not a slogan. That is just what I believe."

— Sumanlata Shrivastav, Founder

In August 2023, we opened with just two computers and a firm belief that rural children deserve digital literacy as much as anyone else. No government scheme, no external funding — just the resolve to make it happen.

Three years on, we have never charged a single student. Boys and girls, school-going children and young adults after Class 12, all sit side by side learning skills that can genuinely change their lives.

Every student who completes a course receives a certificate — because they earned it, and some of them have never received one before.

What We Teach

Practical Skills, Real Outcomes

Every course is designed around what students can actually use — in the workplace, in further studies, or in daily life.

ADCA

Advanced Diploma in Computer Applications

MS Office suite, data entry, internet usage, and practical productivity tools for employment and everyday digital tasks.

HTML / Web Design

Web Design Basics

How websites work, basic HTML page creation, and an introduction to the internet as a tool for learning and opportunity.

Computer Fundamentals

Core Digital Literacy

Hardware, software, typing, and operating systems — the essential foundation that every other skill is built upon.

Who We Serve

Students Who Would Otherwise Be Left Behind

Our students are not from well-off families. This is the only place many of them have ever used a computer.

01

School Students (Age 12–18)

They attend after school hours or on free days. For most, this centre is their first encounter with a computer — and they pick it up faster than you'd expect.

02

Post-Class 12 Youth

Young people at a crossroads, trying to figure out what comes next. Computer skills give them something concrete and marketable to take forward.

03

Girls & Young Women

In villages like ours, girls often miss out on opportunities like this. At our centre, they sit at the same computers and earn the same certificates. No difference.

Our Journey

Three Years of Quiet, Determined Work

We did not grow this centre because we had resources. We grew it because we could not stop.

✦
August 2023

The Beginning

Started with 2 computers, arranged ourselves. The first students came from the village — most of them had never sat in front of a computer before. No announcement, just an open door.

✦
2023–2024

Word Spread Quietly

More students started coming — boys and girls aged 12 to 18, and older students who had passed Class 12 and wanted to learn something useful. Every class remained free.

✦
2024

First Certificates Awarded

Students finished their first courses. Certificates were printed and handed to each student formally on completion day — with mithai and a small ceremony. For some, it was the first certificate they had ever received.

✦
2024–2025

Growing Without Funding

Arranged more computers as demand grew. Now running 7 systems. Still no outside funding. Still no fees charged. Entirely self-sustained through personal commitment.

●
Today

15+ Active Students & Growing

The centre is running. Students are learning. And there are more children waiting — including some we are currently turning away due to limited seats and infrastructure.

Partner With Us

What Your Support Would Unlock

We have done the hard part — built trust over three years, showed up every day, and created a working model. A little support now would let us reach significantly more children.

More Computers or Laptops

We currently have 7 systems for 15+ students. More machines means more children can learn simultaneously, and we can stop turning students away.

Reliable Power Backup

Power cuts interrupt classes mid-session. A proper inverter or UPS setup would ensure no learning session is ever cut short by a power failure again.

Internet Connectivity

Without internet, we cannot access online practice tools, typing software, or the vast resources that could dramatically enhance what we teach. This one addition opens everything.

Computer Maintenance

Keeping our existing machines serviced and functioning well is essential. Regular maintenance support would extend the life of current systems significantly.

Study Materials

Printed notes, reference books, and licensed software that students can use both during and after class to reinforce their learning at home.

We Are Not Looking for a Takeover

We are looking for someone who sees what this already is — a working, trusted, community-embedded initiative — and wants to help it reach more children.
